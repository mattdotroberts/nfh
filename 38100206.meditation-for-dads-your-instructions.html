Here's a few tips before we get started.

  • Most meditation guides will tell you that in order to make it a habit you need to do it at the same time everyday. I know, as a parent, this isn't always possible. But what you can almost always do is to find 10 minutes somewhere. I tend to either do it in the morning (if I'm up before the kids), during a short window in the daytime when they're at school and I'm not in meetings, or in the evening once they've gone to sleep.

  • The practice can be done anywhere, so long as you won't be disturbed mid-way through. Don't feel you need to be sat cross-legged on a cushion. In fact, that can actually do more harm than good when you’re starting out. Try and find somewhere that you won't be disturbed, but don't worry about a little bit of background noise. I tend to use a pair of Airpods and sit in the bedroom, but I've also done it outside on a park bench, or sat parked up in a car before I head back into the house.

  • The benefits of this thing won't be apparently immediate. It's not like you'll sit down for 10 minutes and feel different immediately. But I've found it's a little like compound interest, and the effects get stronger with regular practice.

One last thing before we start this. One of the reasons I've personally found Waking Up to work so well for me (and others I've talked to have shared the same) is that it really has the ability to completely transform how your brain works. This is because it begins to frame the issue of consciousness, where thoughts come from, and the power they have over us, in a way that lead to you re-examine how your brain perceives everything. That's the "waking up" Sam Harris is talking about. The benefit to this shift in worldview is nothing short of momentous.

But it's not something that most people will achieve in just a month. The 28 day course will start to touch on some things that seem a little out of reach out first. But with time, practice and guidance it'll start to open up. So a big piece of advice for those of you who are taking part is to enjoy the next 28 days, not to force anything, and simply try to find 10 minutes a day to look inward.
